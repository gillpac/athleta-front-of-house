Athleta Front of House · v1 Build Scope

Custom lead, trial, sales & retention system  |  Coolaroo & Altona North  |  June 2026 — build brief

What this document is: the build brief for the v1 system, to be developed with Claude Code and deployed on the stack in Section 3. The iterated reception prototype is the frozen reference for screens and interactions; this document defines everything behind and around it. Anything not listed in scope is out of scope for v1.

1 Purpose & success criteria

A purpose-built front-of-house system for Athleta Gymnastics that manages every lead from enquiry to enrolment, drives daily reception activity, processes cancellations through a verified workflow, and reports net growth against target — per site, per person, live. It replaces the Coolaroo spreadsheets, the Zapier lookup interface, and the margin-notes columns on the cancellation sheet.

  • Visibility: every lead's history — who contacted them, when, how, what happened — on one timeline.
  • Action: the system tells each receptionist what to do next; untouched leads escalate visually.
  • Truth: net growth (verified sales − departed cancellations, by effective date) computed in-system, audited by admin verification gates.
  • Accountability: every action stamped with the individual who did it; per-person sales attribution for commission.

5 Lead lifecycle

New lead › Trial booked › Attended › Sale (won) or Didn't enrol → Nurture   |   No-show → re-book (count++)   |   Sale → pending admin → verified
  • Traffic-light rules: not contacted = solid red; new/attention = yellow; booked/good/verified = green; red reserved for problems (no-show, >4h untouched). One orange "next action" button per row.
  • Contact logging: every call records an outcome (no answer / voicemail / spoke—call back / spoke—booking now); texts and emails logged; any outcome sets contacted=true.
  • Booking requires date + time + programme (suggested from age; programme list managed in settings). Re-booking a no-show increments the rebook counter.
  • Sale requires first-class date + class + "rego & insurance payment taken" tick; warns if Jotform outstanding. Sale = pending until admin verifies the iClassPro checklist.
  • Didn't enrol requires a reason (Other = free text) and moves to Lost (dead — moved away, competitor) or Nurture (not now — future follow-up date required); undoable from the profile. All status ticks undoable; undos are logged.
  • Unbooked leads are the priority queue: any lead without a trial booked is surfaced on the Today screen every day — with waiting clock and colour escalation — until it is Booked, Lost or Nurture. The count of unbooked leads per site is a headline number on Stats and in the Monday summary. Leads cannot age out quietly.
  • Mandatory next action: every active lead must carry a next-action date/time until it reaches Enrolled, Lost or Nurture — set automatically by each logged outcome (e.g. "call back later" prompts for when) and surfaced as the daily follow-up list. A lead can never silently have nothing scheduled.

6 Screens — frozen per prototype v10

  • Today: target bar (actual vs goal, to-go, operating-days pace Mon–Sat excl. blockout days) → New leads triage (waiting clock, escalation, call menu) → Today's trials (form status, ① arrived → ② outcome ending in 💰 Make the sale) → Sales to process (checklist + admin verify) → Tomorrow (confirmations + form chasing) → week collapsed.
  • Leads: global search (child/parent/phone/email) + filters (all, received today, not booked, no-shows, nurture, sales) + date ranges; received timestamps inbox-style.
  • Profile (slide-over): action bar (call w/ outcomes, log text/email, book/re-book, make the sale, undo didn't-enrol), programme selector, grouped form controls (pending / resend / got form), original enquiry verbatim, full timeline, add note.
  • Cancellations: Section 8 workflow.
  • Stats: net-growth hero (actual vs target, % bar, per-operating-day pace), lead/trial/sale/no-show/cancellation counts for week + month + custom ranges, My Sales breakdown (clickable, verified vs pending).
  • My shift: action tallies, daily ops checklist (sign-off w/ name + time), personal activity trail.
  • Admin/Exec screens: in scope for v1 but mocked in parallel during the build — verify queues (sales + cancellations), multi-site exec scoreboard, target setting, user management.
  • System settings (backend, management only): blockout days per site (public holidays, shutdown periods — excluded from the operating-days pace calculation automatically); programme/class management — create, rename, archive programmes (Kinder Gym, Beginners Principles, levels, Boys etc.) so class names are never hard-coded; daily checklist editor — checklist items configurable per role and per site (Coolaroo reception sees a different list to Altona Site Leads).
  • Debit schedule (exec): year-ahead view of the fortnightly debit calendar with, for each upcoming debit, the projected active member number — current verified actual extended at the present run rate vs the target trajectory — so you can see whether the next debit lands above or below plan before it happens.

7 The iClassPro discipline

Enrolments and cancellations are entered into iClassPro manually. The system makes this impossible to forget rather than pretending it away:

  • A won lead sits in Sales to process until the checklist (class enrolled · rego & insurance paid · payment details set up) is complete and admin verifies. Unverified sales glow on the admin queue.
  • A cancellation cannot reach Departed until "processed in iClassPro" is ticked and admin verifies + sends the confirmation email.
  • Only verified sales and verified departures count in net growth and commission.

8 Cancellations & retention (upgraded from real form data)

Form received › Notice date verified › Save attempt › Processed in iClassPro › Admin verified + email   →   outcome: Departed / Saved / Paused
  • Form fields captured: child, level (Kinder Gym → Level 4+, Boys classes), site (required), date notified, reason(s) — multi-select, free-text feedback, experience rating 1–5, signature name.
  • System owns the dates: notice date is verified by staff against correspondence (the real data shows parents typing 2015/2035/etc.); effective date = verified notice + 2 weeks, computed, never typed.
  • Outstanding-fees flag: warn when a direct debit falls on/before the effective date (recurring real-world issue in the sheet's margin notes).
  • Multi-child submissions ("Millie & Maeve") split into one record per child; duplicate re-submissions deduped against open records.
  • Paused outcome for holidays/injury/"back next term" — does not count as churn; auto-reminder to follow up at the nominated return window.
  • Reporting: cancellations by reason, by level, by site; average exit rating trend; save rate; notes live on the record (replacing the spreadsheet's margin columns).
